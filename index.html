About Our School

Nebbi's Fastest Rising Star in Education

Sunrise Secondary School is a privately owned co-educational secondary school situated in Nyangama Upper Village, Nebbi Town — at the heart of Uganda's West Nile sub-region. We offer a full secondary education from Senior One through Senior Six, with both day and boarding options.

Under the leadership of Headteacher Denis Ocaki and a devoted teaching staff, Sunrise has rapidly become one of the most respected academic institutions in northern Uganda. Our 2025 CBC results — placing us second in all of West Nile — are proof that excellence in Nebbi is not just possible; it is inevitable.

Academic Programs

Full Secondary Education
from S.1 to S.6

A complete, quality secondary pathway aligned with Uganda's national curriculum — empowering every student to reach their highest potential.

O – Level

Ordinary Level (S.1 – S.4)

Four years of broad secondary education under the UNEB Uganda CBC curriculum. Students master core competencies across Sciences, Humanities and Technical subjects, sitting the Uganda Certificate of Education (UCE) at S.4.

Mathematics Sciences English SST / RE Performing Arts ICT
A – Level

Advanced Level (S.5 – S.6)

Two years of specialist study for the Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE). Students choose subject combinations aligned to their university and career goals — from Sciences to Arts to Business.

PCB / PCM HEG / MEG Economics General Paper Literature
Co-Curricular

Boarding, Sports & Life Skills

Beyond the classroom — our boarding students benefit from structured evening preps, sports, music, drama, debates and community service. We build whole students, not just exam passers.

Football Athletics Music & Drama Debates Leadership
CBC Excellence

Leading West Nile in Competency-Based Learning

Uganda's Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC), introduced for lower secondary in 2020, shifts education from rote memorisation to practical, skill-based learning. Sunrise Secondary School has embraced CBC fully — and the results speak for themselves.

Our top student Emmy Munguacel scored 6 A's, 2 B's and 1 C — the best result in the entire West Nile region. Precious Wanguic attributed her success to research, project work and guidance from supervisors — exactly the skills CBC is designed to build.
